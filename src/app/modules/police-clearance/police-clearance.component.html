A police clearance certificate is used by Entelect to provide to a potential client in the Netherlands. This is just a cautionary step needed in the immigration process.

Timeline

This usually takes up to 8 weeks.

Take note!

Get this done early in the process, but this document is only valid for 6 months after it is issued. So you don't have to get this done first thing, but it is easy to forget about.

I don't think the 6 months validity period is a hard-deadline for this document, so I would not be too stressed about this. But, please make sure that you do get it before coming over.

You will have to collect this document from the Police Station where you applied for it yourself. If for any reason you can not collect it, you have to specify the person who would be collecting it. Upon collection, whoever collects it would have to provide their proof of Id.
Getting your police clearance certificate
Visit your nearest SAPS police station

Make sure to bring your identification documents, such as your South African ID card or passport.

Request a police clearance certificate application form (or SAPS 91(a))

Fill out the form accurately and completely. You may need to provide personal details, including your full name, date and place of birth, fingerprints, and previous addresses.

Fingerprints

At the police station, they will guide you on the fingerprinting process. Usually, your fingerprints will be captured electronically using a fingerprint scanner.

Pay the required fee

The cost for a police clearance certificate can vary, so it's best to inquire about the current fee at the police station. the fee is around R 150, but it is subject to change.

Submit the application

Hand in your completed application form, along with the necessary supporting documents, fingerprints, and the payment receipt. The police station will guide you on the submission process and provide you with a reference number for tracking your application.
